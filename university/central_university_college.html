The Kwame Nkrumah University Of Science and Technology, which started as the Kumasi College Of Technology in the early 1950's, recieved its accreditation as a University in 1961 and it is the second largest tertiary institution in Ghana. It is located in Kumasi, Ghana's second largets city. It is coeducational and offers courses in diverse fields. However, the distinguishing featuresof this institution is that is focuses on the teaching of science and technology, as well as research and information dssemination in the same area.


All first degree programmes are of four(4) years duration. In particular it's Engineering Programmes are highly competitive, and it's fame rests on the high demand and quality of output in this area. Total student population in 2008/2009 was about 24,924 comprising of 15,698 males and 6,552 females. In 2007/2008 it was about 24,001 and 2004/2005 academic year it was about 18,580Admission is very competitive for all courses and open to both local and foreign students. For example, just 39% of all applicants for undergraduate courses got admission in 2007/2008. That is 17,438 students applied for admission and only 6,368 were admitted. In 2005/2006 16,463 qualified students applied for asmission but only 8,594(including 470 foreign students) gained admission. In 2004/2005, it was estimated that about 13,000 people applied for admission, out of which only about 6,580 were taken.


In 2007/2008, outof the total number that applied for admission, 11,848 were males and 5,590 were females. Out of the total applications, 4,923 males and 1,445 females were offered admission. Also in 2007/2008, 1,954 students applied for postgraduate programmes and 1,387 got admission. Unlike the undergraduate level the number that got admission for postgradutae studies represented as much as 71% of the total applicants. In the same year, 443 foreign students applied and 277 were offered admission.During this period, 243 from Less Endowed Schools also obtained admission. The total number of Ghanaians admitted as fee-paying students in 2007/2008 was as much as 634, comprising of 406 males and 228 females. This wasan interesting development and presented a good case for the ability of Ghanaian public tertiary institutions to be self-financing in future.


There are about 741 academic staff in 2008/2009. Like all public Universities in Ghana, is has campus residential facilities for students and lecturers, however due to the increased intakes, a lot more are non-residential.For the 2009/2010 admissions, the following new courses were advertised: B.Sc Real Estates, B.Sc Quantity Surveying and Construction Economics(Building Technology), B.Sc Construction Technology and Management(Building Technology), B.Sc Planning, B.Sc Veterinary Medicine (BVM). B.Sc Biomedical Engineering, B.Sc Meteorology and Climatic Science, B.Sc Agricultural Biotechnology, B.Sc Dairy and Meat Science and Technology, B.Sc Landscape Design and Management.
