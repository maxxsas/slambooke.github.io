University Of Ghana Legon



Founded in 1948, the University of Ghana is the oldest and most renowned tertiary institution in Ghana. It is located in Accra, Ghana's capital city. The mission of the University is to develop world-class human resource and capacity to meet national needs through quality teaching, learning, and research and knowledge dissemination. It offers courses in very diverse fields and the standards of its programmes are universally acclaimed. It is co-educational and admission is very competitive for all courses and also open for both local and foreign students. In 2005 total student population was 26,959 (Legon, Korle-Bu and Accra City) and total staff population was 3,759.

In 2005/2006 19,624 students applied and about 9743 were admitted while in 2004/2005 academic year, 19,700 candidates applied for admission and 11,057 were offered admission. It has campus residential facilities for most students and lecturers, however, due to sharp increase in student intake in recent times, lots of students are non-residential.

Legon is particularly known for its Medical, Business and law schools and these have neck-breaking competitions for entry. The University has also phased out admission via the WAEC's G.C.E "O" and "A" Levels with effect from the 2007/2008 academic year. such candidates are expected to apply through the Accra City Campus as Mature Students.

In order to preserve, maintain and enhance the nuclear knowledge in Ghana, the University of Ghana has also established a school in Nuclear and Allied Sciences at Atomic, Kwabenya in Accra in 2006, in collaboration with the Ghana Atomic Energy Commission and the International Atomic Energy Agency.
